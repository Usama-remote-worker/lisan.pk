## Why Certified Translation Matters for Official Documents

One of the biggest mistakes applicants make is assuming any translation is acceptable for official use. **It is not.**

Embassies, universities, immigration departments, and legal authorities in Pakistan and abroad often require a strict set of credentials:
1. **Certified translation** statement of accuracy
2. **Translator stamp** verifying agency registration
3. **Official signature** from an authorized translator
4. **Official formatting** mirroring the layout of original documents
5. **Terminology accuracy** mapping technical and legal terms correctly

## Why Embassies Reject Poor Translation

This is one of the biggest fears applicants have. At Lisan.pk, we frequently receive urgent cases where users already faced rejection because of poor translation quality.

### Incorrect Terminology
Embassies carefully verify legal wording, educational terminology, names, dates, and formatting consistency. Machine translation or inexperienced translators often create serious errors that look highly unprofessional.

### Passport Spelling Mismatch
Even a single letter spelling inconsistency between your passport, translated document, and visa paperwork can create weeks of delays or outright rejection. We cross-verify all translated names with your official passport.

### Missing Certification
Many rejected translations lack a registered stamp, official signature, translator declaration, or a formal statement of accuracy. Official documentation always requires professionally certified translation.

### Poor Formatting
Formatting matters more than most users realize. Incomplete layouts, missing seals, and inconsistent formatting commonly trigger objections. Our translations mirror the original document layout so reviewers can easily cross-reference them.
