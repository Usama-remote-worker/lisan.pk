Embassies and legal authorities often reject documents because of simple, avoidable errors:

## Which Documents Usually Require Certified Translation?

Different industries require different translation standards.

### Educational Documents
Students commonly require translation for:
* Degrees and transcripts (HEC-recognized)
* IBCC equivalence certificates
* Scholarship paperwork and study plans
* Recommendation letters

Saudi scholarship applications require exceptionally precise Arabic terminology and formatting.

### Legal and Court Documents
Legal translation requires maximum precision. Examples include:
* Affidavits (unmarried status, identity verification)
* Court orders and legal judgments
* **Nikah Nama (Marriage Certificate)**
* Divorce certificates (Talaq Nama)
* Power of attorney (Wakalatnama)

Even one spelling mistake on a legal certificate can create massive legal inconsistencies.

### Visa and Immigration Paperwork
Immigration-focused translation commonly includes:
* Passport documents
* **Police character certificates**
* Employment and experience records
* Birth certificates (NADRA Computerized)
* Financial sponsorship letters

According to [Statista](https://www.statista.com/), global migration continues increasing annually, creating growing demand for certified document translation worldwide.

### Commercial and Business Documents
Businesses often require translation for:
* Commercial contracts and agreements
* Commercial invoices and Bills of Lading
* Import/export documents
* Company registration paperwork (SEC / Chamber of Commerce)

Commercial translation requires both legal and industry-specific terminology expertise.
