## What Are Official Translation Services in Pakistan?

Official translation services refer to certified, stamped, and signed translations accepted by embassies, universities, and government authorities like HEC and MOFA.

### Certified vs. Normal Translation
Here’s where most people make a mistake:
*   **Normal Translation**: Just converted text. This is **NOT** accepted by authorities.
*   **Certified Translation**: Includes an official stamp, signature, and verification statement. This is **REQUIRED**.

If your translation is not certified, it’s useless for [Saudi scholarships](/blog/required-documents-saudi-scholarship-2026), visa applications, and degree equivalency.

## Why Stamped & Signed Translation Matters

Saudi institutions require proof that the translation is accurate, done by a recognized provider, and officially verified. Without a stamp and signature, your document is considered invalid for official review.

For the 2026 fully funded scholarship cycle, translation is mandatory. Universities like **King Saud University (KSU)** and **King Abdulaziz University (KAU)** require Arabic translations of all academic documents, including:
