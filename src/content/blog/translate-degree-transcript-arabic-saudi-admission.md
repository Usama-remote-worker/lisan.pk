If you're applying to a Saudi university, one thing is non-negotiable: your **degree and transcript must be translated into Arabic** correctly. 

Many Pakistani students miss deadlines or get rejected simply because their translations are not certified or not accepted by the [Saudi Ministry of Education](https://moe.gov.sa/). This guide explains exactly how to translate your academic documents — and how to do it fast, correctly, and without risk.

## Why Arabic Translation Is Required for Saudi Admission

Saudi universities and government institutions process all official records in Arabic. While some preliminary portals might allow English, the final admission and visa stages almost always demand certified Arabic versions.

Even if your degree is originally in English, most institutions still require:
1.  **Official Arabic translation**
2.  **Certified formatting** (mirroring the original)
3.  **Verified content accuracy**

> [!IMPORTANT]
> Without proper [certified Arabic translation](/services/translation), your application may be marked as incomplete and permanently closed the moment the deadline passes.

## Which Documents Must Be Translated

When applying for admission or scholarships, you usually need a full set of academic credentials translated:

*   **Degree Certificate** (Bachelor’s / Master’s / PhD)
*   **Academic Transcripts** (Detailed Marks Certificates)
*   **Matric & Intermediate Certificates** (SSC/HSSC)
*   **Passport or ID copy**
*   **Experience Certificates** (if required for professional degrees)

Submitting incomplete translations is one of the most common reasons for rejection in the 2026 cycle.

## Requirements for Certified Arabic Translation

To be accepted by King Saud University, King Abdulaziz University, or the [Islamic University of Madinah](https://admission.iu.edu.sa/), your translation must meet strict standards:

*   **Accurate word-for-word translation**: No paraphrasing of technical terms.
*   **Official certification**: A translator’s official stamp and signature on every page.
*   **Matching formatting**: The Arabic version must mirror the original document layout exactly.
*   **Professional registration**: Done by a provider recognized for [Saudi scholarship document translation](/blog/saudi-scholarship-document-translation).

> [!CAUTION]
> Cheap or informal translations from unverified freelancers often fail these requirements and lead to immediate rejection.

## Common Mistakes That Lead to Rejection

Avoid these critical errors if you want your application to succeed:
*   **Using Google Translate**: Academic terminology requires human precision.
*   **Hiring non-certified freelancers**: They lack the required official stamps.
*   **Missing official certification statement**: Every translation must include a "Certificate of Accuracy."
*   **Incorrect formatting**: If the transcript layout is different, committees may find it hard to verify.
*   **Submitting partial documents**: Always translate the full transcript, including the grading key.
