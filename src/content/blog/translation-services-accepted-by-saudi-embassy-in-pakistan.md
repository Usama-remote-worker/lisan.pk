If your documents are not translated correctly, the Saudi Embassy will reject your application—no warning, no second chances.

That’s why finding translation services accepted by Saudi embassy is critical for Pakistani students applying for visas, scholarships, or university admissions. In this guide, you’ll learn exactly what the embassy requires, which documents need translation, and how to avoid costly mistakes.

## Why Saudi Embassy Requires Certified Translation

The Saudi Embassy only accepts documents translated into Arabic by certified and recognized translators.

This is because:

*   Arabic is the official language of Saudi Arabia
*   Legal accuracy is mandatory
*   Documents must match original meaning word-for-word

If your translation is not certified, stamped, and professionally formatted, it can be rejected instantly.

## What Documents Need Translation for Saudi Arabia

### Educational Documents

Students applying for scholarships or universities must translate:

*   Degrees
*   Transcripts
*   Certificates
*   Equivalence letters

These must follow Saudi-approved formatting standards.

### Legal & Personal Documents

You may also need:

*   Birth certificate
*   Marriage certificate
*   Police clearance certificate

All must be translated into Arabic and properly stamped.

### Visa & Immigration Documents

For Saudi visa applications:

*   Passport copies
*   Medical reports
*   Visa forms

Accuracy here is critical—errors can delay or cancel your visa.

## Requirements for Translation Accepted by Saudi Embassy

To ensure your documents are accepted, your translation must:

*   **Be in Arabic language**
*   **Include a certified translator stamp**
*   **Match the original document exactly**
*   **Be formatted professionally**
*   **Include translator credentials**

> [!CAUTION]
> Anything less = high risk of rejection.

## Common Mistakes That Lead to Rejection

Most students make these mistakes:

*   Using Google Translate or freelancers
*   Missing official stamp
*   Incorrect Arabic terminology
*   Formatting issues
*   Unverified translation agencies

These errors can cost you time, money, and even your scholarship opportunity.
