Document attestation is a crucial step for anyone planning to travel, work, or study abroad. Whether you are applying for a visa, seeking employment, or pursuing higher education, attested documents are often a mandatory requirement. In this guide, we will walk you through the process of document attestation in Pakistan, specifically focusing on requirements for Saudi Arabia.

## Why is Attestation Necessary?

Attestation verifies the authenticity of a document. Foreign governments and organizations need assurance that the degrees, marriage certificates, or birth certificates you present are genuine. The attestation process involves a chain of verifications starting from the issuing authority up to the Ministry of Foreign Affairs (MOFA) and the relevant embassy.

## The Chain of Attestation

For most documents, the process follows this general hierarchy:

1.  **Issuing Authority:** The document must first be verified by the institution that issued it (e.g., University, Board of Intermediate and Secondary Education).
2.  **Higher Education Commission (HEC) / IBCC:** For educational documents, HEC (for degrees) or IBCC (for matric/inter certificates) attestation is required.
3.  **Ministry of Foreign Affairs (MOFA):** Once verified by the educational bodies, the document is attested by MOFA.
4.  **Embassy Attestation:** Finally, the document is attested by the embassy of the country you are visiting (e.g., Saudi Embassy).

## Specific Requirements for Saudi Arabia

The Royal Embassy of Saudi Arabia in Islamabad has specific requirements for attestation. 

*   **Degree Attestation:** Requires prior attestation from HEC and MOFA.
*   **Marriage/Birth Certificates:** Require prior attestation from MOFA.
*   **Translation:** Documents often need to be translated into Arabic before embassy attestation.
