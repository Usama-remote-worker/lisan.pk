## Overcoming Common Translation Pitfalls (FAQs)

**Can I use automated tools like Google Translate or DeepL for my visa application?**
Automated tools are excellent for quick, informal phrase checks. However, they lack the legal authority required for official submissions. They cannot issue a signed Certificate of Translation Accuracy, nor can they capture handwritten text on complex government stamps. Relying on automated software for official applications can result in immediate rejections or RFE letters.

**Do my Spanish translations need to be notarized for the embassy?**
For standard Spanish visa paths and Ministry of Foreign Affairs (MOFA) legalizations, a formal translation certified by an authorized agency is usually sufficient. A separate notary public stamp is generally only necessary if your specific document is being submitted for specialized judicial proceedings or explicit court-ordered validations.

**How do you handle name spelling discrepancies?**
This is a frequent issue with cultural names across varying registries. Our expert translators ensure that the English or Spanish rendering reflects the original record exactly, but we can append an official translator's note to explicitly align the text with the spelling on your valid passport, preventing processing holdups.
