## Why the UK Home Office Rejects Self-Translated NADRA Documents

Many applicants mistakenly assume that if they are fluent in both Urdu and English, they can translate their own documents or have a bilingual family member do it. This is a critical mistake that results in immediate documentation failure.

According to official UK Government translation rules, the Home Office explicitly states that all submitted translations must be executed by an independent, professional third-party translator or translation agency. The UKVI caseworkers must be able to independently verify the credentials of the professional behind the text.

> **Industry Insight:** Leading industry reports show that over 15% of visa application delays are directly caused by non-compliant, uncertified, or poorly translated foreign documents.

To meet the legal threshold for 2026 UK visa guidelines, every single translated page must feature a formal "Certificate of Accuracy." This certificate is legally required to display four critical components:

- [x] An explicit declaration stating it is a "true and accurate translation of the original document."
- [x] The exact date of the translation.
- [x] The translator's or translation agency representative’s full name and physical signature.
- [x] Verifiable contact details (phone number, email, and office address) so Home Office entry clearance officers can quickly verify authenticity.

Moreover, latest metrics reveal that nearly 60% of professional translation agencies cite mismatched name spellings between Urdu source text and English passports as the primary reason for client revisions. When you choose a dedicated provider of true translation services, you completely eliminate these bureaucratic vulnerabilities.
