## Strict Federal Rules: What USCIS Mandates for Translated Documents

Many applicants assume that if a NADRA birth certificate is partially bilingual, it can be submitted to immigration officers as-is. This is a hazardous misconception. Under federal regulation **8 CFR § 103.2(b)(3)**, USCIS explicitly demands that any document containing foreign language text must be accompanied by a complete, word-for-word English translation that a professional translator has fully certified.



To fulfill the rigorous standard of a **true translation**, your submitted document must include an independent "Certificate of Translation Accuracy." According to official USCIS translation guidelines, this certificate is a legal declaration that must explicitly feature:

1. A clear statement of **competence**, proving the translator is fluent in both Urdu and English.
2. An **accuracy statement** affirming the English text is a complete and faithful translation of the original record.
3. The translator's or agency representative's **printed name, physical signature, and date**.
4. Full, verifiable **contact details** (phone number, email, and company address) on formal letterhead.

Furthermore, immigration experts warn that a major pitfall causing case delays is the omission of marginal annotations. A legally compliant translation must capture every single structural element—including governmental seals, localized registration numbers, handwritten notes by the local Union Council, and hidden stamps.
