## Why Translation Services in Pakistan Are Not All the Same

Not all translation is equal. The difference lies in **acceptance**. 

Standard translation involves merely swapping words from one language to another. Certified translation, however, carries legal weight.

### Certified vs Uncertified Translation
* **Certified translation** includes an official stamp, translator signature, registration numbers, and a formal declaration of compliance. This is mandatory for legal and official bodies.
* **Uncertified translation** (including informal freelance work or printouts) lacks official credentials and is immediately rejected by embassies, foreign universities, and government bodies.

### Embassy Approved vs Regular Translation
Embassies require structured formatting that mirrors the original layout, precise technical vocabulary, and rigorous verification of names against passports — not just basic language conversion.

### Why Small Mistakes Cause Rejection
Even minor errors can derail your plans:
* **Name spelling mismatch:** A difference of a single letter between your translation and passport.
* **Wrong Arabic terminology:** Translating legal status, degree titles, or stamps literally instead of using formal equivalents.
* **Missing certification:** Submitting translated pages without a registered seal or a signed statement of accuracy.
