## 1. Why Standard Translations Fail UK Home Office & UKVI Audits

Many applicants make the costly mistake of uploading translations produced by unverified freelancers or generic automated software. In the United Kingdom, the Home Office applies a rigid verification standard to all incoming foreign language files.

### The Official UK Certification Rule

According to [UK government guidelines on certifying a document](https://www.gov.uk/certifying-a-document), a valid translation must include a formal, signed declaration from the translation company. This declaration must explicitly state:

* That it is a **true and accurate translation** of the original document
* The **exact date** the translation was completed
* The **full name and contact details** of the translation agency representative
* The **credentialed status** of the professional translation provider

If your submitted papers lack this formal verification block, the UKVI portal will flag your profile — immediately stalling your visa timeline or triggering an outright application denial.
