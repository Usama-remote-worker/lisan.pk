## The Legal Standard: Decoding 8 CFR § 103.2(b)(3) for Urdu Records

A common pitfall for many bilingual applicants is attempting to translate their own civil files or relying on raw automated tools. However, according to the strict letter of federal law under **8 CFR § 103.2(b)(3)**, any document containing foreign language text submitted to USCIS must be accompanied by a comprehensive English translation that a professional translator has formally certified as complete and accurate. Self-translations are an automatic grounds for document rejection.



To meet the legal threshold for a true translation that passes federal fraud-prevention screenings, your document pack must include a standalone "Certificate of Translation Accuracy." As outlined by modern USCIS document translation rules, this certificate must explicitly feature:

- [x] A clear statement affirming the translator's fluency and competence in both Urdu and English.
- [x] A declaration that the translation is complete and accurate to the best of their knowledge.
- [x] The translator's or agency representative's printed name, physical signature, and date.
- [x] The translating body's corporate contact details (phone number, email, and physical office address).
