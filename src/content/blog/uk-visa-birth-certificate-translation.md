Applying for a UK visa with a non-English birth certificate? Learn UKVI birth certificate translation requirements, costs, timelines, and how to avoid common mistakes that can delay your application.

Applying for a UK visa often requires supporting documents that prove your identity, family relationship, and personal history. One of the most commonly requested documents is a birth certificate.

If your birth certificate is not in English or Welsh, UK Visas and Immigration (UKVI) requires a certified translation before it can be properly considered as part of your application.

Whether you're applying for a Student Visa, Spouse Visa, Skilled Worker Visa, Dependent Visa, or Settlement route, understanding the requirements for UK visa birth certificate translation can help you avoid delays and ensure your application meets UKVI standards.

In this guide, you'll learn exactly when a translation is required, what UKVI expects, common mistakes to avoid, costs, and how to obtain a compliant certified translation.

---

## Do You Need a Birth Certificate Translation for a UK Visa?

In most cases, yes.

UKVI requires that documents submitted in support of an immigration application be in English or Welsh. If your birth certificate is issued in another language, it must be accompanied by a certified translation.

This applies to applicants from countries including:
* Pakistan
* Saudi Arabia
* UAE
* India
* Turkey
* Egypt
* Morocco
* Indonesia
* Bangladesh
* China

...and any other country where the original document is not entirely in English or Welsh.

A translated birth certificate allows UKVI caseworkers to verify important information such as:
* Full name
* Date of birth
* Place of birth
* Parent details
* Registration information

## UKVI Requirements for Birth Certificate Translation

A translated birth certificate must meet specific standards.

A compliant translation should include:

### Certification Statement
Confirmation that the translation is accurate and complete.

### Translator Details
The translator's:
* Full name
* Signature
* Contact information

### Translation Date
The date on which the translation was completed.

### Complete Translation
The entire document should be translated. This includes:
* Stamps
* Seals
* Notes
* Handwritten annotations
* Registration numbers

Missing information can create unnecessary complications during review.

## What Should a Certified Birth Certificate Translation Include?

A professional UKVI-compliant translation package generally contains:

### 1. Original Birth Certificate Copy
A scanned copy of the original document.

### 2. Complete English Translation
Every visible element translated accurately.

### 3. Certification Statement
A declaration from the translator confirming accuracy and competence.

### 4. Translator Information
Identification and contact details of the translator or translation company.

## Common Birth Certificate Translation Mistakes

Many applicants unknowingly make errors that can delay processing.

### Using Machine Translation
Tools such as Google Translate may not correctly interpret official terminology, stamps, or handwritten information.

### Self-Translation
Applicants often ask whether they can translate their own birth certificates. Because UKVI expects independently verifiable translations, professional certified translation services are strongly recommended.

### Missing Certification
A translation without a certification statement may not satisfy UKVI requirements.

### Incomplete Translation
Every section of the document should be translated.

### Poor Scan Quality
Blurred scans can lead to mistakes and omissions.

## Certified Translation vs Notarized Translation

A common misconception is that UKVI requires notarized translations.

In most situations, what matters is that the translation is certified and includes the required translator declaration. Notarization serves a different legal function and is not automatically required for immigration applications.
