## Attestation After Translation – Do You Need It?

Yes. After you receive the certified Arabic translation, you must get it attested in this order:

1.  **Notary Public** (in your city)
2.  **Ministry of Foreign Affairs (MOFA)**, Islamabad ([official MOFA website](https://mofa.gov.pk/))
3.  **Higher Education Commission (HEC)**, Pakistan ([official HEC website](https://www.hec.gov.pk/)) (Required prior for educational docs)
4.  **Saudi Cultural Attaché** (or Saudi Embassy, depending on scholarship type)

We can either deliver the translation ready for attestation or handle the first two steps for you (notary + MOFA) at an additional fee. The Saudi Cultural Attaché final attestation usually requires your personal presence.

*Key takeaway: Without attestation, your translation is just a piece of paper. With full attestation, it becomes a legal document for Saudi authorities.*
