## Why Your Saudi Scholarship 2026 Application Requires Arabic Translation

Saudi Arabian universities and the Ministry of Education operate primarily in Arabic. Even if your original degrees are in English, the scholarship committees require a certified Arabic translation to verify your academic standing and identity. Providing a high-quality translation isn't just a formality—it is a core part of your "Your Money or Your Life" (YMYL) documentation that proves your eligibility.

## Critical Documents That Must Be Translated


Before you hit "submit" on your portal, ensure the following documents have a professional Arabic counterpart:

- **Educational Degrees**: Matric, Inter, Bachelor’s, or Master’s certificates.
- **Academic Transcripts**: Detailed mark sheets showing your GPA and course credits.
- **Police Clearance Certificate**: Required for your visa and initial university screening.
- **Medical Fitness Reports**: Must be translated to prove you meet health standards.
- **Birth Certificates/B-Form**: Essential for identity verification.
- **Recommendation Letters**: Often required in Arabic if not originally issued in English/Arabic.
