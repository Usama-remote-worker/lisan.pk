## Certified vs Notarized vs Attested Translation — What’s the Difference?

This is one of the most misunderstood areas in Pakistan. Knowing the difference protects you from ordering the wrong document set.

### Certified Translation
Certified translation is a translation accompanied by a formal, signed, and stamped statement from the translator or translation agency. It declares that the translated text is a true and accurate representation of the original. This is commonly required for **embassies, universities, immigration, and scholarships**.

### Notarized Translation
Notarization involves a separate legal verification procedure through an authorized Notary Public. The Notary Public verifies the identity of the translator and signs/seals the translation to witness the signature. Some foreign authorities specifically request notarized translation for legal or international use.

### MOFA & Embassy Attestation
Attestation and legalization are completely separate processes connected to document verification. The original document must first be attested by local bodies (such as HEC or IBCC), then both original and translated copies are attested by the **Ministry of Foreign Affairs (MOFA)** and finally by the target embassy itself.
