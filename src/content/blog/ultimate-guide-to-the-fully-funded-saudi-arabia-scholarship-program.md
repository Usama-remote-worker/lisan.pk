## 💰 The Financial Matrix: What Does the Fully Funded Package Cover?

When public educational systems talk about a "fully funded" ride, few match the exhaustive scope provided by Saudi government scholarships. The financial framework is explicitly structured to eliminate economic barriers so you can focus entirely on your academic performance. 

> **Data Insight:** According to official data from the Ministry of Education, selected international scholars receive 100% financial coverage. Statistical analysis shows that reducing living-cost pressure correlates with a 24% increase in research output among international postgraduate cohorts. 

The financial coverage includes:
* **Full tuition fee waivers:** Complete exemption from all academic, admission, and registration fees. 
* **Regular monthly stipend:** A standard living allowance (typically 2,000 SAR for select tiers, and 900–1,500 SAR baseline) to cover personal daily costs. 
* **Free furnished accommodation:** Safe, modern on-campus university housing including utilities, internet, and regular maintenance. 
* **Comprehensive healthcare:** Full medical and dental insurance coverage at top-tier university hospitals. 
* **Annual round-trip airfare:** Paid air tickets from your home country to Saudi Arabia every academic year. 
* **Preparation allowance:** A financial reward upon arrival to help you settle into your new academic environment smoothly.

## ⚖️ Eligibility Criteria: Do You Qualify?

Before filling out your online application form, you must verify that you satisfy the strict age and baseline operational thresholds set across all 27 public universities. The government categorizes applicants into **internal scholarships** (for non-Saudi students already legally residing inside the Kingdom) and **external scholarships** (for students applying from abroad, such as from Pakistan). 

### Age Boundaries by Degree Level
* **Undergraduate Programs:** Applicants must be between 17 and 25 years old. 
* **Master’s Degree Level:** Candidates must typically be under 30 years old. 
* **PhD Level Research:** Applicants must generally not exceed 35 years old. 

Beyond age limits, you must maintain a clean criminal record, provide a validated medical fitness certificate showing no infectious diseases, and ensure you have never been previously dismissed from any educational institution within Saudi Arabia.

## 📂 The Document Checklist: Ensuring Zero Errors

A single missing, un-attested, or poorly translated document is the **number one reason** applications are rejected during the initial automated screening phase. Treat this checklist as your absolute baseline before submission.

### Step 1: Gather Academic Records
Compile your official academic transcripts and graduation certificates. All documents not originally in English or Arabic must be translated into English or Arabic by licensed translation agencies. 
* *Pro Tip:* Check our comprehensive guide on [Required Documents for Saudi Scholarships](/blog/required-documents-saudi-scholarship-2026) to make sure you have everything ready.

### Step 2: Secure Legal Attestations
Obtain attested academic certificates from your local Ministry of Education (like the **Higher Education Commission (HEC)** in Pakistan) and, crucially, get them certified by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs (MOFA) and the local Saudi Embassy or Cultural Attaché in your home country.
* *Need help with attestations?* Read our deep dive into parsing [Saudi Embassy Attested Document Translation Process](/blog/saudi-embassy-attested-document-translation-process-pakistan) for international students.

### Step 3: Draft Core Identity Documents
Ensure your passport copy is valid for at least one full year. Prepare recent passport-sized photographs (with white background) and a clean, concise professional CV.

### Step 4: Acquire Endorsements & Proofs
Request two recommendation letters from former professors or faculty members. If applying for an English-taught tech or engineering tract, attach your English language proficiency test scores (such as an IELTS score of 5.0+ or TOEFL iBT of 61+).

For Master’s and doctoral applicants, your **Statement of Purpose (SOP)** holds immense algorithmic and human weight. Selection committees look for clear research potential that intersects cleanly with the Kingdom's developmental goals.
