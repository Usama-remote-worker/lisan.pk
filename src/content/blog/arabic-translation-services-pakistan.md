## Key Documents That Require Official Arabic Translation in Pakistan

You cannot take just any document straight to a translator's desk. According to current Ministry of Foreign Affairs (MOFA) Guidelines, your files must first receive a primary verification stamp from the specific local authority that issued them. Once that stamp is secured, they must be legally translated into Arabic before final submission.

The most common files everyday applicants need to convert include:

### 1. NADRA Marriage and Birth Certificates
This includes your [NADRA Birth Certificate](/services/translation/birth-certificate-translation), [Marriage Registration Certificate (MRC / Nikah Nama)](/services/translation/nikah-nama-marriage-certificate), and Family Registration Certificate (FRC). These are mandatory for family sponsorship and residency visas. They must be signed and stamped by the secretary of your local union council before translation.

### 2. HEC Degrees and Educational Transcripts
If you are moving for a professional job or a university scholarship, your Matric and Intermediate certificates must be verified by the IBCC, and your higher degrees must carry a fresh stamp from the [Higher Education Commission (HEC) through our attestation services](/services/attestation).

### 3. Corporate and Commercial Contracts
For businesses expanding into Riyadh or Dubai, files like Power of Attorney, company registration certificates, and trade agreements must be verified by the Chamber of Commerce before moving to the linguistic phase.

### The Threat of Layout and Right-to-Left (RTL) Formatting Issues
Because Arabic reads from right to left, automated tools regularly break document formatting. They misalign official seals, flip critical registration dates, or misinterpret specific legal phrasing. Government offices do not accept digital "guesses"—they require authorized human signatures and physical stamps.
